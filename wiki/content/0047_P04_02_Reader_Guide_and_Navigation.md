# Reader Guide and Navigation

**How to Move Through the CDS Wiki**

---

## Welcome

This wiki is deliberately large and layered. It is not designed for quick scanning. It is designed for those who want to spend time with the project and let the mythology, music, and visuals accumulate meaning.

This guide exists to help you navigate without getting lost.

## Recommended Paths

### For First-Time Deep Readers
1. Start with **The Project Overview** and **Philosophy and Core Themes**
2. Read the three main **Band Member** pages
3. Read the **Forge and the Sisters** overview
4. Read **The Misalignment** and **Digital Displacement**
5. Listen to **My Digital Requiem**, **Beautiful Disgrace**, and **Heart of the Static** while reading their deep-dive pages
6. Explore the **Symbolism Dictionary** and **Recurring Motifs**

### For Music-Focused Readers
- Use the **Discography** page as a starting point
- Follow individual **Song Deep Dive** pages
- Cross-reference with the **Character** pages when a song feels tied to one member’s perspective

### For Mythology-Focused Readers
- Read the full **Mythology** section in order
- Pay special attention to **The Temporal Trinity Myth** and **The Architect and the Forge**
- Use the **Glossary** (when available) for recurring concepts

### For Visual / Aesthetic Readers
- Start with **Visual Identity and Worldbuilding**
- Move into **Colors and Anchors** and **Recurring Visual Motifs**
- Look at how environments and motifs support the themes

## How Pages Are Connected

Most pages contain cross-links to related characters, songs, or concepts. Following these links is one of the best ways to experience the wiki’s density. The project is built on resonance and interconnection — the wiki reflects that.

## A Note on Density

You are not expected to read everything in one sitting. This wiki is meant to be returned to over time. Many pages gain additional meaning after you have spent time with the music and visuals they reference.

There is no “correct” order. There is only the path that feels alive to you at the moment.
