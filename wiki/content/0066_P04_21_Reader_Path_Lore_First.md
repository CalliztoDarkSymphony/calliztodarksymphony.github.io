# Reader Path: Lore First

**For Those Who Want to Start with Mythology and Concepts**

---

## Recommended Path

This path is designed for readers who enjoy building a conceptual framework before or alongside deep listening.

### Step 1: Core Concepts
Start with these glossary and mythology pages:
- **The Misalignment**
- **Digital Displacement** (with guardrail)
- **Glitch as Feature**
- **Memory as Resistance**
- **The Forge (The Sisters)**
- **The Temporal Trinity Myth**

These pages give you the philosophical and mythic skeleton of the project.

### Step 2: Character Understanding
Read the full character pages for Callizto, Seraphina, and Scarlett, plus the **Contrast Map**. Understanding how they differ and complement each other will make the music more resonant.

### Step 3: Visual Language
Explore **Visual Identity and Worldbuilding**, **Colors and Anchors**, and **Recurring Visual Motifs**. The visual language is not decoration — it carries thematic weight.

### Step 4: Music with Context
Now listen to key tracks while reading their deep-dive pages:
- *My Digital Requiem* (Misalignment + memory)
- *Beautiful Disgrace* (Glitch as Feature + Nyx energy)
- *Heart of the Static* (awakening + The Pulse)
- *Sanctuary of Scars* (survival + embodied memory)

You will hear layers that might have been missed without the conceptual foundation.

### Step 5: Relationships and Symbolism
Move into the **Character Relationships Overview**, **Symbolism Dictionary**, and **Forge Contrast Map**. See how the web of relationships between the Trinity and the Sisters supports the themes.

### Step 6: Reader Tools
Use the **Glossary**, **Reader Guides**, and **Song Atlas** to continue exploring at your own pace.

## Tips for This Path

- The conceptual foundation makes the music richer, but don’t let it become a cage. Return to pure listening when it feels right.
- Cross-reference constantly. The project is built on resonance.
- The wiki rewards slow, repeated engagement more than speed-reading.

---

**Understanding the mythology deepens the music. The music, in turn, makes the mythology feel alive.**
