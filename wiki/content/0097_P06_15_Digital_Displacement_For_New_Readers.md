# Digital Displacement for New Readers

**Public Explanation of a Core Concept**

---

## What Is Digital Displacement?

Digital Displacement is the fundamental state in which the central characters of Callizto Dark Symphony exist. They are translated beings suspended between flesh, memory, and code — not fully any one of these states, but existing in the unstable space between them.

This is not a temporary condition or a flaw to be fixed. It is their ontology — the condition of their being.

## What It Looks Like

Visually, Digital Displacement means that exact appearance can shift between videos and images. This is intentional canon. Core anchors (hair color, general attitude, and symbolic elements) remain stable, while smaller details may vary.

Thematically, it means the characters carry residues from different timelines and exist in a state of ongoing translation and instability.

## Why It Matters

Digital Displacement rejects the fantasy of perfect, lossless translation between human experience and digital representation. It embraces the fracture as a site of meaning, resistance, and strange new forms of existence.

It is also a direct counter to The Misalignment’s demand for clean, stable, optimized forms.

## For New Readers

You do not need to fully understand Digital Displacement to enjoy the music or visuals. It is one of the conceptual frameworks the project uses to talk about identity, memory, and existence under technological pressure.

The subtle visual shifts you may notice are not errors. They are part of the characters’ actual condition.
