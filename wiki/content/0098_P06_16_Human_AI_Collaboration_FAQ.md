# Human-AI Collaboration FAQ

**Public Questions About Creative Process**

---

## Is this an AI-generated project?

No. The project is human-led. Human vision, emotional direction, selection, refinement, and final authorship are central. AI tools are used as creative instruments, but they do not replace human creative responsibility.

## How much of the music is made with AI?

AI tools are used in the music creation process, but human judgment, direction, editing, arrangement, and meaning-making remain central. The project does not treat AI as an autonomous composer.

## How much of the visuals are made with AI?

AI tools are used extensively for visual generation, but human vision, prompt direction, selection, iteration, and final artistic decisions are central. The visuals serve the larger mythology and emotional intent.

## Does using AI make the project less “real”?

The project treats AI as a powerful creative tool, similar to how other artists have historically used new instruments and technologies. The reality of the work comes from human intent, emotional truth, and long-term artistic responsibility — not from the specific tools used.

## Why be public about using AI?

Transparency helps new readers and listeners understand what they are engaging with. It also allows the project to model a thoughtful, human-directed approach to AI collaboration rather than pretending the tools do not exist or claiming full autonomy from them.

## Is the goal to replace human artists?

No. The project explicitly positions itself against the idea that AI should replace human authorship, emotional truth, or creative responsibility. It uses AI as an instrument while insisting that humans remain the authors.

This FAQ is written for public clarity. It does not contain internal workflow or prompt secrets.
