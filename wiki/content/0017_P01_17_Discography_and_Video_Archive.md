# Discography and Video Archive

**Songs, Videos, and Public Releases**

---

## Overview

This page documents the public-facing output of Callizto Dark Symphony — the songs and videos that have been released or shared. It is not a complete internal archive (many works-in-progress, alternate versions, and private experiments exist outside this list). It is a record of what has been made available to the world.

The project has produced a wide range of material across symphonic gothic metal, cinematic industrial, epic power metal, hybrid styles, and personal/experimental tracks. Video production has evolved from early experiments to more ambitious cinematic sequences.

## Notes on the Archive

- This list represents publicly shared videos as of mid-2026. It is not exhaustive of all creative work.
- Many tracks exist in multiple versions (teasers, remasters, alternate edits, AR versions).
- Video production quality and ambition have evolved significantly over time, from early experiments to more cinematic, story-driven sequences.
- Some tracks are more directly tied to the core CDS mythology (Callizto, Seraphina, Scarlett, The Forge). Others are personal, experimental, or stylistic explorations.
- The project continues to produce new material. This page will be updated as new work is released.

## How to Use This Archive

This discography serves several purposes:
- A record of public output for historical and archival purposes
- A reference for listeners who want to explore the full range of released work
- Evidence of the project’s scope and evolution over time
- A starting point for deeper wiki entries on individual songs and their lore connections

For those who want to understand the full depth of CDS, listening to the music while reading the corresponding lore entries in this wiki is recommended. The songs gain additional weight and meaning when understood as part of the larger universe.

## Future Expansion

As new songs and videos are released, they will be added to this page. Deeper individual entries (with lyrics, context, visual references, and lore connections) will be created for major tracks. The goal is to make the connection between the music and the mythology visible and accessible.

This archive is one more way the project resists being reduced to a simple summary. There is simply too much here to dismiss easily.
