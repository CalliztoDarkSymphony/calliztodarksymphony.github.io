# CDS FAQ for New Readers

**Frequently Asked Questions**

---

## What is Callizto Dark Symphony?

It is a human-led, AI-assisted creative project combining original dark symphonic music, mythic storytelling, and a gothic/cyberpunk visual language. It centers on a fictional digital band (the Temporal Trinity) within a larger developing mythology.

## Do I need to understand the mythology to enjoy the music?

No. Many people enjoy the music on its own. The wiki and mythology are there for those who want to go deeper into the characters, themes, and world behind the songs.

## Is this an “AI-generated band”?

The band members are fictional/digital in structure, but the project is human-directed. Human vision, emotional judgment, selection, and final authorship are central. AI tools are used as instruments, not as autonomous creators.

## What kind of music is it?

Dark symphonic, gothic, industrial, and hybrid metal styles with cinematic and atmospheric elements. The music often explores heavy emotional and thematic territory.

## Who are Callizto, Seraphina, and Scarlett?

They are the three members of the fictional digital band known as the Temporal Trinity. Each has a distinct identity, visual anchor, and thematic role within the project’s mythology.

## Why is there a big wiki?

The wiki serves as a public archive and reference for the project’s mythology, characters, songs, and themes. It exists for listeners and readers who want deeper context. It is optional — not required reading.

## Is everything in the wiki “canon”?

The wiki distinguishes between protected canon facts (locked for consistency) and working/fluid elements that can evolve. Not everything is fixed; some parts are intentionally open to interpretation or development.

## Where should I start?

Listen to some music first. Good entry points include *My Digital Requiem*, *Beautiful Disgrace*, *Heart of the Static*, and *Sanctuary of Scars*. Then explore the wiki if you want more context.

## Is this project finished?

No. It is an ongoing, expanding body of work. New music, visuals, and wiki content continue to be added over time.
